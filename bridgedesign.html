Designing a Steel Bridge
...From Wood






The fall of my Junior year at The Cooper Union I took a Structural Analysis course that involved designing and building a steel bridge. Yet, these photos are obviously not that of a steel bridge. During the design phase my group members and I came up with an over-designed first attempt. Rather than try and change the design to make less members or fewer connections we decided to look into using a weaker material. The rules of the project did not state that we had to use steel, so based on the load the bridge needed to handle (600 lb point load applied at the center) we selected pine wood.








Instead of using steel we decided to use wood. The reasons for this was that the competition had price, performance, and weight as the deciding factors in our overall score. Wood is going to be lighter and cheaper in this type of design and price and weight were two categories that our design ended up being the most efficient in.



The bridge had a span of six feet and was constructed out of pine wood, sheets of aluminium, steel bolts, and two steel cables. The basic design premise of the model was a truss arch bridge, with steel cables to take tensile forces.
